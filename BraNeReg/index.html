Brain Network Regulation (BraNeReg)

A study about learning to regulate your own brain activity

What is it about? #

In this multi-session study, you will be trained to regulate your own brain activity by means of Neurofeedback. During Neurofeedback, activity in brain networks of which you are usually not aware, is recorded and shown to you in real-time. This immediate feedback will make you aware of this brain activity and allows you learn to control it.

Once you have learned how to control your brain networks, we are interested in how this novel skill affects cognition and affect, and we will ask you to perform several tasks inside and outside of the MRI scanner, while we take a variety of physiological measures (heart rate, respiration, skin conductance). There will be 6-7 MRI sessions and 2 behavioural sessions, of varying lengths.

We are further interested in how being able to regulate these brain networks affects your mood and physiology in daily life, and we will ask you to answer short daily questionnaires and wear a wristband that records physiological data (hear rate, skin conductance, physical activity) for 3 weeks.

Your participation in the study will be spread over 7 weeks in which you will visit the Donders Centre for Cognitive Neuroimaging 8-9 times in total.

You will be reimbursed 190–200 € for your participation!
