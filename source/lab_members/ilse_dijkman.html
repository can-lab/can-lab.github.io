Ilse Dijkman

Trainee

I've always been curious about the human mind and how we become the person we are today. I have a broad interest in neuropsychology and I'm fascinated by many questions ranging from 'why does someone develop anxiety' to 'how can we build long-lasting resilience'. But my main interest has always been how stress affects us and how we can help prevent or cure psychopathologies.

Right now, I'm an internee at the MindRest-project. Together with Nikos, we will investigate the underlying mechanisms of a Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) training in healthy but stressed students. During my internship I will mainly focus on how MBSR improves stress resilience, and which personality traits serve as a predictor of better health outcomes.

This internship is part of my Master's studies Biomedical Sciences. I've finished my Bachelor's degree here in Nijmegen during which I completed the minor Neuroscience of Stress-Related Psychopathologies. It was during this minor that I knew for sure I wanted to continue this direction by choosing the Medical Neuroscience specialisation in the Master. Before this internship, I wrote a literature thesis on Sensory Processing Sensitivity and how to build resilience under the supervision of prof. dr. Judith Homberg.

In my free time I love to be creative! I like to write, draw, do yoga and dance. I'm absolute dreamer and I aim to become a Science Journalist one day to spread my enthusiasm about science. Hopefully, you'll see my name one day in the Quest, Kijk, or Volkskrant.
