Tim Vriens

Research Assistant

I am a master’s student in the Cognitive Neuroscience program. Currently, I am working on my master’s thesis, researching the effects of stress on decision-making. My research interests are in the fields of stress, decision-making, motivation, computational modelling, learning and memory. Previously, I completed a Bachelor’s program in Mathematics and Biomedical Sciences, where I did an EEG-fMRI combined study to the role of the prefrontal cortex in prediction and prediction error. Furthermore, I did some research to methods for clustering recordings of neuronal activity.

In my free time I like to practice all kinds of sports, especially fencing.
